How to: CWI Balancing

Use this dashboard to balance payroll, rent, insurance, banking, and store pricing around your CWI.

What is CWI? Your Classroom Wage Index represents the average weekly income students can expect to earn.

How it's calculated:

CWI = Pay Rate × Expected Weekly Hours

Example:

  • Pay rate: $10/hour
  • Expected hours: 5 hours/week
  • CWI = $50/week
paymentsWhy CWI Matters: Use it to balance your economy. Rent should be 20-40% of CWI; store items should generally range from 10-200% of CWI depending on desirability.
settingsSetup: If payroll is not configured yet, set it up first to unlock CWI calculations.

Update pay rate and expected weekly hours in Payroll, then return here to review the resulting CWI and recommendations.

  • Class selector: choose which class period you are reviewing. Each class has its own CWI based on its payroll settings.
  • Payroll inputs: pay rate and weekly hours are managed in Payroll and feed the CWI shown here.
  • Balance snapshot: flags mismatches between CWI and costs so you can adjust settings.
Side effects: Saving Payroll changes updates the CWI and downstream balance guidance for the selected class.

Review the balance snapshot to see how rent, insurance, fines, and store prices compare to CWI.

Watch for flags: Large gaps suggest a setting may be too high or too low.

Review your current CWI and see how payroll, rent, banking, insurance, and the store fit together.
