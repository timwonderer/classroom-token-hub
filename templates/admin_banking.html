Monitor student balances, review transactions, and configure savings settings.

What you'll see:

  • Total Checking — All money available for immediate spending
  • Total Savings — Money set aside earning interest
  • Total Deposits — Combined checking + savings across all students
  • Students w/ Savings — How many students are actively saving

Example: 20 students with $10 in checking and $5 in savings equals $300 in total deposits.

Teaching Tip: Encourage at least 30% of students to use savings accounts. This teaches delayed gratification and financial planning!

Use the Transactions tab to review deposits, withdrawals, transfers, and fees for each student.

  • Filter by student, account type, or date to investigate a specific concern.
  • Common entries include payroll deposits, rent charges, store purchases, and savings transfers.

Configure savings interest, payout schedules, and overdraft rules in the Settings tab.

Rate changes affect future payouts only and will not re-calculate past interest.

Reminder: Setting changes apply moving forward and do not alter past transactions.

Basic Settings:

  • Savings APY: Annual percentage yield (e.g., 5% APY means about $0.42 earned per $100 saved monthly)
  • Interest schedule: How often students earn interest
    • Weekly: Frequent rewards, ideal for younger students
    • Monthly: Mirrors real banking, better for older students

Advanced Settings:

  • Overdraft protection: When enabled, savings automatically covers checking shortfalls (recommended for beginners)
  • Overdraft fees: When protection is off, charge a fee if balance goes negative to reinforce consequences
Side effects: Rate changes only affect future interest, not past payments. Interest schedule changes take effect on the next scheduled run.
Recommended Setup: Start with 5% APY, weekly payouts, and overdraft protection enabled. Adjust based on student engagement.
