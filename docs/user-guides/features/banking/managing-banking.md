# Managing Banking Settings

Customize how the banking system works for your students, including interest types, rates, and detailed schedules.

## Critical Settings

### 1. Interest Configuration
Encourage saving by paying interest on balances.

- **Savings APY**: The annual percentage yield. A 5% APY means students earn approximately 5% of their average balance over a year.
- **Interest Type**: Choose how interest is calculated:
    - **Simple**: Interest is calculated only on the principal amount. Easier for younger students to understand.
    - **Compound**: Interest is calculated on the principal *plus* any previously earned interest. This demonstrates the power of long-term saving.
- **Interest Interval**: How often interest is paid (Weekly, Monthly, etc.). Frequent payouts (Weekly) can be more engaging.

### 2. Per-Block Configuration
Banking settings are independent per class block. Use the **Class/Block Selector** to:

- Set different interest rates for different classes.
- Experiment with Simple interest in one block and Compound in another to compare results.

### 3. Protection & Fees
- **Overdraft Protection**: Automatically transfer funds from Savings to Checking to cover expenses. Recommended for new economies.
- **Overdraft Fee**: Charge a fee if a student's balance drops below zero (when protection is off).

## Educational Tips
- **Compare Interest**: Run a simulation or use different settings in different blocks to show the difference between Simple and Compound interest.
- **High Rates**: Verification economies often use hyper-inflated interest rates (e.g., 5% *monthly* instead of annually) to make the growth visible within a semester.

## Troubleshooting
- **Interest not paying?** Check the "Next Payout" date in the settings.
- **Calculation seems wrong?** Remember that APY is annualized. A 5% APY paid weekly pays roughly 5% / 52 each week, not 5% every week.
